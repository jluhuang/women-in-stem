Mary G. Ross

August 9, 1908 - April 29, 2008

The first Native American woman engineer, Mary G. Ross was one of the founding members of the Lockheed Corporation's Advanced Development Programs, code-named "Skunk Works," where she worked on preliminary design concepts for interplanetary space travel, space flight, ballistic missiles, orbiting satellites, and flyby missions to Venus and Mars. She is featured on the 2019 U.S. Sacagawea dollar commemorating Native Americans' contributions to the space program.
