Mae Jemison

October 17, 1956 - present

Mae Jemison is an American doctor, engineer, and former NASA astronaut who became the first black woman to travel into space when she was selected for the STS-47 mission in 1992, which remained in orbit for almost eight days. Since leaving NASA in 1993, she has written several children's books, established a technology research company and a non-profit educational foundation, and taken over leadership of DARPA's 100-Year Starship program
