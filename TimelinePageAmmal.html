Janaki Ammal

November 4, 1897 - February 7, 1984

The first female Indian plant scientist, Janaki Ammal helped to develop a number of hybrid crop species that are still grown today, including varieties of sweet sugarcane and eggplant. Her work in cytogenetics advanced our knowledge about breeding and evolutionary patterns of botanical groups. Ammal is remembered today as a pioneer in the fields of botany and cytology and a passionate advocate of nature conservation.
